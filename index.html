Abraham Lincoln

Abraham Lincoln, An average boy from Kentucky that makes history.

Born in 1809, in Kentucky, Abraham Lincoln went on to become the 16th President of the United States of America.

  • Feb. 12 1809 - Born in Hardin County (now LaRue County), KY
  • Fall of 1816 - Family moved to Pigeon Creek, Indiana
  • 1819-1824 - Attended school when available
  • Mar. 1830 - Moved with family to Macon County, Illinois
  • Apr. 7 1832 - Elected captain, Thirty-first Regiment, Illinois Militia, in the Black Hawk War
  • 1834 - Began studying law
  • Aug. 4 1834 - Elected as Whig candidate from Sangamon County to the lower house of the Illinois General Assembly
  • Sept. 9 1836 - Became licensed to practice law
  • 1836-1842 - Reelected for three additional terms as the Sangamon County representative of the Illinois General Assembly. He was considered one of the "Long Nine" in the Sangamon delegation and played an instrumental role in moving the state capital to Springfield.
  • Mar. 7 1849 - Admitted to the bar of the United States Supreme Court
  • May 18 1860 - Nominated at Republican Party candidate for president
  • Nov. 6 1860 - Elected President of the United States
  • Apr. 12 1861 - Civil War startes as a result of confederates in Charleston firing upon Union-held Fort Sumner
  • Jan. 1 1863 - Issued Final Emacipation Proclamation, freeing slaves from rebellious states
  • Nov. 19 1863 - Delivered the famous Gettysburg Address
  • Nov. 8 1864 - Reelected POTUS
  • Apr. 11 1865 - Delivered final public speech
  • Apr. 14 1865 - Shot by John Wilkes Booth, while attending a theater performance, succumbing to his injuries at 7:22 a.m the following day
  • Apr. 19-20 1865 - Funeral held at the White House
  • Oct. 15 1874 - Permanent Lincoln Tomb dedicated

All is high hope and confidence.

--Abraham Lincoln
